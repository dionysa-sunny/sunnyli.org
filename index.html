Working papers

Glass Ceiling Unshattered: Asymmetric Gender Culture among Immigrants in German Tracks

Working paper, 2026

Pools two starting cohorts of the German National Educational Panel (NEPS) to estimate value-added models of math achievement by school track. Immigrant girls in Gymnasium face a compounded disadvantage; in comprehensive schools the pattern reverses. Matched-sample and structural estimation of Penn (2017)’s piecewise threshold model localize the aspiration trap to a single demographic-by-institution cell: Gymnasium immigrant girls.

Social learning and democratic transition

Working paper, 2026

A social-learning model that derives three equilibrium regimes — turnover, bandwagon, and entrenchment — from the interaction between civic confidence and elite screening. Empirical applications to the democratic transitions of Japan and Taiwan. The project connects information economics to normative questions about what democratization requires beyond elections.

Status recognition and international conflict

Working paper, 2026

A relational re-examination of Renshon (2016). Replaces the community-rank status-deficit measure with a dyadic recognition construct derived from the diplomatic exchange network, and shows the conflict result does not survive once recognition is modeled directly rather than through community-level ordinal ranks.

Party screening and elite competition in young democracies

Working paper, 2026

An extension of the social-learning framework that endogenizes party screening costs and characterizes when elite competition sustains turnover versus locks in entrenchment. Applications to Meiji Japan and post-martial-law Taiwan.
