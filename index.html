Working papers

Glass Ceiling Unshattered: Asymmetric Gender Culture among Immigrants in German Tracks

Working paper, 2026

Pools two starting cohorts of the German National Educational Panel (NEPS) to estimate value-added models of math achievement by school track. Immigrant girls in Gymnasium face a compounded disadvantage; in comprehensive schools the pattern reverses. Matched-sample manifest similar patterns.

paper (PDF) · slides

Social learning and democratic transition

Working paper, 2026

A social-learning model that derives three equilibrium regimes — turnover, bandwagon, and entrenchment — from the interaction between civic confidence and elite screening. Empirical applications to the democratic transitions of Japan and Taiwan. The project connects information economics to normative questions about what democratization requires beyond elections.
